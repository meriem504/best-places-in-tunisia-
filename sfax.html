Sfax

Sfax is a city located in the eastern part of Tunisia, specifically in the Gulf of Gabes. It is the second-largest city in the country and serves as the capital of the Sfax Governorate. Sfax is an important economic and industrial center, known for its thriving port, textile industry, and olive oil production.
1.The city has a rich history that dates back to ancient times, and it boasts a well-preserved medina (old town) with narrow streets, historic buildings, and traditional architecture. The medina of Sfax is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and offers a glimpse into the city's past.
2.Sfax is also known for its vibrant fish market, where visitors can find a variety of freshly caught seafood. The city's port plays a crucial role in Tunisia's trade, serving as a hub for exports and imports.
3.In addition to its economic significance, Sfax offers cultural attractions such as museums, art galleries, and theaters. The city hosts various festivals and events throughout the year, celebrating local traditions and art forms.
4.Furthermore, Sfax is a gateway to the nearby Kerkennah Islands, a group of small islands known for their picturesque landscapes, sandy beaches, and traditional fishing villages. These islands are a popular destination for tourists looking to relax and enjoy the natural beauty of the area.
Sfax can be reached by air through the Sfax-Thyna International Airport or by train, as it is well-connected to other major cities in Tunisia.
